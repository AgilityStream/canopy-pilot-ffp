The Problem Everyone Knows

Your Students Get 5 Minutes of Canopy Time Per Jump. It's Not Enough.

Every instructor knows the gap. Students exit the aircraft, deploy, and then have roughly five minutes to practice the single most consequential skill in the sport — navigating their canopy to a safe landing.

Five minutes. No do-overs. No pause button. And if they make a mistake, the consequences are real.

~5 minutes
Average canopy flight time per jump — the only live practice a student gets for the highest-risk phase of every skydive.

Ground school helps with theory. Classroom videos show the concepts. But nothing prepares a student for canopy decision-making like actually flying a canopy — feeling the toggle pressure, reading the wind, judging the landing pattern.

Until now, there was no way to give students that experience without putting them in the air.

The Cost of Doing Nothing

Every Season Without Better Canopy Training Is a Season You're Betting Against the Odds

The problems DZ owners and training directors deal with every season — and the costs that come with them.

⚠️

Landing Incidents

Canopy-related injuries remain the leading cause of skydiving accidents. Students struggle with pattern recognition, flare timing, and wind judgment — skills that take dozens of jumps to build through experience alone.

📋

Liability Exposure

Every student injury creates legal, insurance, and reputational risk. Training programs that can demonstrate additional safety measures are in a fundamentally stronger position.

🌧️

Weather Days Kill Progress

A week of bad weather doesn't just delay training — it resets student confidence and muscle memory. Ground holds mean lost revenue and frustrated students who never come back.

🔄

Slow Student Progression

Students who progress slowly cost more instructor time, generate less revenue per student, and are more likely to quit before completing their license.

The FFP study put it simply: "If this investment prevents even a single parachute accident per season, its price will then, without the slightest hesitation, be fully justified."
